Street Children in Accra

Research conducted by the Department of Social Welfare in Ghana indicates that there are 33,000 children living on the streets in the country. The street children are left to fend for themselves with resultant problems such as teenage pregnancies, children growing up and becoming social misfits and engaging in social vices such as arm robbery, drug addiction etc.

Street Children in School

According to the Country Representative of the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) in Ghana, Dr Iyabode Olusanmi, 75 per cent of all street children live in Accra and Kumasi, with 71 per cent of them being illiterates.

Street Children in Ibadan

There are a few NGOs in Ghana trying to help in addressing this social problem in the society. Some are addressing the issue of street children by providing regular and free counseling for them when they are in trouble or to prevent them from getting into trouble. Others make the effort to enroll them back to school by paying their fees and providing their uniforms and other associated educational costs.
